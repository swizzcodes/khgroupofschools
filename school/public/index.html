Brief history of the school



KH Matriculation School was established in 1991 by Janab Mohamed Hashim Saheb, an industrialist and philanthropist, with the vision of the holistic child development. Initially starting with 16 staff and 89 students, the school expanded quickly, gaining state recognition in 1994 and extending to higher grades annually. In 1996, a seperate boys school campus was establised in Then Nandiyalam. Over time, KH Matriculation School evolved into KH Group of Schools, featuring distinct campuses for boys and girls, KH Matriculation Boys Higher Secondary School and KH Matriculation Girls Higher Secondary School. By 1998, the group was elevated to Higher Secondary School status. As of the time recorded, the Boys School has 570 students and 60 staff, while the Girls School has 1707 students and 150 staff. On August 14, 2013, the institution was certified as Muslim Minority Institution by the National Commission for Minority Educational Institutions. KH Group of Schools is now recognized as a leading institution in Ranipet district, known for quality education over three decades.
